Exploring the Aquatic Insects of Colorado

Welcome

Colorado Chironomids is dedicated to the study and documentation of midges (Chironomidae) found in Colorado's aquatic ecosystems.

Chironomids are incredibly important indicators of water quality and play a vital role in aquatic food webs throughout the state.

About Chironomids

Chironomidae, commonly known as midges or non-biting midges, are a family of insects closely related to mosquitoes. However, unlike their cousins, adult chironomids do not bite.

These insects are found in nearly every aquatic habitat and are essential food sources for fish and other aquatic organisms.

Colorado's Waterways

Colorado's diverse aquatic ecosystems—from mountain streams to high-altitude lakes—support a remarkable diversity of chironomid species.

This project aims to document and study these fascinating insects across the state.
